## Data and R-script behind "When AI turns grant evaluation into a lottery”

When referring to or reusing these materials, **please cite** the corresponding [preprint](https://doi.org/10.31222/osf.io/d8gcu_v1) and this repository [<sup>1</sup>](#1).

### **Content**

[Data](https://github.com/MartinBulla/correspondance_funding/tree/main/Data/) - contains data.csv with data collated from [EU Funding & Tenders Portal](https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/) from the official reports. These reports are published annually by the European Research Executive Agency (REA) following the conclusion of the evaluation process. While individual project scores are confidential, these public summaries provide the aggregate percentages of proposals meeting or exceeding specific quality thresholds. **Accessing the raw reports**: (i) navigate to the EU Funding & Tenders Portal -> Calls for proposal, (ii) search for the specific call (e.g. HORIZON-MSCA-2024-PF-01-01), (iii) look for the data, usually under the "Topic conditions and documents" or "Updates" section.

[R](https://github.com/MartinBulla/correspondance_funding/tree/main/R/) - contains Fig_1.R R-script that generates Fig_1.png as well as the median values reported in its figure captions.

[Outputs](https://github.com/MartinBulla/correspondance_funding/tree/main/Output/) - contains PNG file of Fig. 1.

Data Sourcing & Transparency
The scoring distribution data for the Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Postdoctoral Fellowships (PF) were aggregated from the official "Flash information on the overall results of the call" reports.

These reports are published annually by the European Research Executive Agency (REA) following the conclusion of the evaluation process. While individual project scores are confidential, these public summaries provide the aggregate percentages of proposals meeting or exceeding specific quality thresholds (e.g., the 70% threshold for the 'Seal of Excellence' and the 85-95% ranges for funding).
